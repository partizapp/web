## What Is Never Have I Ever?

Never Have I Ever is a social party game where players take turns making statements starting with "Never have I ever…". Anyone in the group who *has* done the thing puts down a finger, takes a drink, or loses a point — depending on how you play. The goal is a mix of learning about each other and strategically crafting statements that reveal surprising truths.

---

## The Basic Rules

**You will need:** 3–10 players (more is fine with the right setup)

**How to play:**

1. Everyone starts with 10 fingers up (or 5 for a shorter game).
2. On your turn, say "Never have I ever…" followed by something you have never done.
3. Anyone who *has* done it puts one finger down.
4. The first person with no fingers left is out — or just for fun, declared the winner of life experience.
5. Play continues around the circle until someone is out or you run out of statements.

**Alternative scoring:** Use points instead of fingers. Anyone who puts a finger down gains a point. Highest score at the end wins — or loses, depending on how you look at it.

## Variations

### Drinking Game Version
Rules are the same — anyone who has done the thing takes a drink instead of putting a finger down. Works best with drinks that are easy to sip slowly. Always play responsibly.

### Kids / Family Version
Stick to the Mild tier. Focus on funny and lighthearted statements. Skip points entirely and just use it as a "raise your hand" game. Great for road trips.

### Speed Round
Set a 10-second timer per statement. If you can't think of one in time, you put a finger down automatically. Keeps the energy high.

### Team Version
Split into two teams. Each team takes turns making statements about the other team. Points go to the team with fewer fingers down at the end.

## Tips for a Great Game

- **Mix your statements** — alternating mild and wild keeps everyone on their toes.
- **React, don't just reveal** — when someone puts a finger down for something surprising, let people share the story if they want. That's where the real fun is.
- **Know your crowd** — always read the room. A statement that's funny in one group might land awkwardly in another.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many players do you need for Never Have I Ever?**
The game works with as few as 2, but it's much more fun with 4–8. Bigger groups can break into smaller circles.

**What if no one has done the statement?**
The person who said "Never have I ever…" puts their own finger down as a penalty for a bad statement. This keeps people strategic.

**Can Never Have I Ever be played online?**
Yes — it works over video call. Use a screen-shared list or an app that handles the question rotation automatically.

**Is Never Have I Ever the same as drinking Never Have I Ever?**
The core game is the same. The drinking variant just replaces finger-counting with sips. You can always play without drinks and it's just as fun.
